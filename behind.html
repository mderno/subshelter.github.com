subshelter.org

i used to use a certain social media app that begins with an "i" and ends with an "m", for sharing my life and collecting memories. on a bad day one of my favourite things to do, was go down the memory lane of things that captured my attention, of good food i ate, and good times i've had with my friends. that was in an earlier version of internet, the one where we all saw the same content at the same time.

i miss chronologicality, i am tired of the algorithm(s).

i used to say i grew up on the internet; for a small town girl the internet (right after the books) was a window to the world that could offer something more than my local peers could. it was a light at the end of the tunnel, that gave me hope.

i, too, can escape this small town and do something big and exciting one day.

i'm saddened by the fact i grew such distance and resentment towards the world-wide-web and this is my way of reviving my desire of sharing my thoughts and views on the internet. i don't know how often and when i will do it; i might do it a lot, i might not do it at all— but i will surely do either.

if you somehow ended up here—welcome. this will be a project in ongoing development. you're invited to check in every once in a while to see what's up.

peace, love, unity & respect
