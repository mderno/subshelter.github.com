subshelter.org

i used to say i grew up on the internet. for a small town girl who was lucky enough to have a pc at home since i was aware of my existence the internet was a window into a life, greater of what my local peers and my environment could offer. it gave a little girl from sevnica, slovenia a chance to see and be heard. it was a light at the end of the tunnel, it was the future.

i,   too,   can escape this shithole  and   do something  big    and exciting   ...

... one day      

being on the internet in early 00s was like opening doors of the advent calendar, except there was no limit* to how many doors you can open on which day, and each time you opened one, another dozen appeared. i spent hours deep diving wikipedia wormholes and random niche fanblogs, i picked up my first html words through pimping my tumblr themes, sustained multiple long distance penfriendships, hacked software i couldn’t buy, conducted my first beats in fruity loops, and designed my first posters in photoshop cs3.

i remember when youtube was at its peak, when facebook overrode netlog, when vine disappeared, and i remember very well when instagram was born. the latter was the one that excited me the most. she was the cool girl next door—she was simple, she was pretty, she was artsy, and she was also so nice. she was ... perfect. no reels, no explore page, no stories, no messaging, no algorithms—only square images. of your friends. chronologically. on the feed. remember there was a time when we all got the same results if we googled the same phrase—it was raw, organic, unoptimised. more sincere and somehow more credible. funnily, even with less security checks people somehow acted more rightfully and truthfully on the internet back then. (or maybe that's just the sentiment i refuse to let go of)

we know what the internet is today and going deeper into that makes no sense for this piece of writing. only thing i have to add is that i’m saddened by the fact i grew such distance and resentment towards the world-wide-web i once thought of so highly. in the past years i often felt confused and anxious regarding my online presence, and consequently acted extremely bipolar on social media. i slowly stopped sharing content for pure joy and i’ve come to realise i’m not okay with that. this is my way of reviving my desire of having my own piece of virtual land on the internet. a land where no idea is too stupid, where each voice can echo, and the freedom of speech lives without compromise rent free—naturally avoiding and fighting against any forms of hate speech and exclusion. on our land we live by peace, love, unity, and respect.™

if you somehow ended up on this page and made it to the end of this piece—welcome. this will be a project in ongoing development. i don't know how often and when i will update it; i might do it a lot, i might not do it at all— but i will surely do either. you're invited to check in every once in a while to see what's up.
